About

This is the unofficial website of The Alan Turing Institute's Space Science Interest group.
For the official website see here.

We plan to run monthly seminars aimed at early-career researchers to showcase their leading work in the fields of astronomy, astrophysics, planetary science, and any space-related topics. We also plan to run community workshops and hackathons. More on this below.

We aim to:

  • Foster collaborative approaches and exchange of ideas within The Turingâ€™s growing astronomy community. Run interactive discussion workshops, hackathons and conferences.
  • Provide a unified forum to engage with other interest groups on large astronomical data projects.
  • Provide a platform for the wider UK and international AstroML community to engage with the Turing community.
  • Organise unified responses to grant calls and sessions at leading conferences.
  • Promote early-career researchers (through talks/colloquia) and promote ML in astronomy as a career path in the UK
